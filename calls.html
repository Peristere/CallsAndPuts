What is a Call Option?

Definition: A call option gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy an underlying asset at a specified strike price before expiration.

Call options are typically used when investors expect the price of the underlying asset to increase. Buying a call allows them to potentially purchase at a lower price than the future market value.

How Call Options Work

When you buy a call option, you’re betting that the asset’s price will rise above the strike price before the option expires. If it does, you can exercise the option to buy at the lower strike price and potentially sell at a profit.

Understanding Graph Trends for Call Options

Graph trends in call options are often associated with price movement patterns. Here are a few key trends:

  • Uptrend: A continuous increase in price over time, ideal for call options.
  • Breakout: Price breaking through a resistance level, signaling potential further rise.
  • Reversal: When a downtrend reverses, it can indicate a buying opportunity.
  • Consolidation: Price movement in a narrow range, often leading to a breakout.
