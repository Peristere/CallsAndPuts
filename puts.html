What is a Put Option?

Definition: A put option gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell an underlying asset at a specified strike price before expiration.

Put options allow investors to profit if they expect the asset’s price to decline, enabling them to sell at a higher, locked-in price.

How Put Options Work

When you buy a put option, you’re betting that the asset’s price will drop below the strike price before the option expires. If it does, you can exercise the option to sell at the higher strike price and potentially repurchase at a lower market price.

Understanding Graph Trends for Put Options

Graph trends in put options often align with certain price movement patterns. Here are key trends:

  • Downtrend: A continuous decrease in price, ideal for put options.
  • Reversal: A shift from an uptrend, signaling potential selling opportunities.
  • Support Break: When price falls below a support level, indicating potential further decline.
  • Consolidation: A narrow price range, which could precede a breakout.
